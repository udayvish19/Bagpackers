Clock Tower

The clock tower stands tall, surrounded by the lively Sardar Market, which bustles with colorful stalls showcasing handicrafts, textiles, and spices. The area is a sensory experience, immersing visitors in Jodhpur’s vibrant heritage. The clock itself was sourced from London and is believed to be from the same company that built the famous clock tower in London.

Located in the heart of Jodhpur, Ghanta Ghar, also known as the Clock Tower, is a historic landmark and a prominent feature of the city’s skyline.Built during the reign of Maharaja Sardar Singh (late 19th century to early 20th century), the tower was initially a major landmark near the Mehrangarh Fort.

Address

Nai Sarak, Ghantaghar Market, 342001, Jodhpur
Coordinates
26.2929° N, 73.02452° E

History

The tower was built by Maharaja Sardar Singh, from whom the adjacent Sardar Market takes its name. An ancient clock tower stands tall, preserved in the middle of the city, believed to be the sole source for tracking time for locals in earlier times. The top of this tower gives an amazing panorama of the city.

Additional Information

- Visitors can climb the tower for a panoramic view of the city.
- The area is popular for shopping and dining, with a range of local cuisine options available.
- The clock tower is a popular spot for photography, especially during sunset and nighttime when it is illuminated.

In summary, The Clock Tower in Jodhpur, also known as the Ghanta Ghar, is a vibrant and historic landmark situated in the heart of the city’s bustling Sardar Market. Constructed in 1911 by Maharaja Sardar Singh, this iconic structure is renowned for its impressive clock and ornate design that blends Rajput and Victorian architectural styles. Surrounded by lively markets filled with colorful textiles, spices, and local crafts, the Clock Tower offers visitors a unique blend of cultural vibrancy and historical significance. Its central location makes it a perfect starting point for exploring Jodhpur’s rich heritage and vibrant street life.
