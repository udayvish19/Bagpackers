Balsamand Lake

Balsamand Lake is a lake situated 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) from Jodhpur on Jodhpur-Mandore Road. This lake is a popular picnic spot, by Balak Rao Pratihar in 1159 AD who belongs to the kshatriya community. It was designed as a water reservoir to provide water to Mandore. The lake has a length of one kilometre (0.62 mi), breadth of 50 metres (160 ft) and a depth of 15 metres (49 ft).

Balsamand Lake Palace

The Balsamand Lake Palace was built later as a summer palace on its shore. The lake is surrounded by lush green gardens that house groves of trees like mango, papaya, pomegranate, guava and plum. Animals and birds like the jackal and peacock also call this place home

In summary, Balsamand Lake, nestled in the scenic foothills of the Aravalli range, offers a serene escape with its tranquil waters and lush surroundings. Ideal for nature lovers and peace seekers, the lake features picturesque views, boating opportunities, and bird-watching spots. Its peaceful ambiance and the nearby historic Balsamand Palace make it a charming destination for relaxation and exploration.
